## Free for most people

You can use ARN v9 **for free** if:

- You're an individual, student, researcher, or hobbyist
- You work for a company with **fewer than 100 people** (employees + contractors combined)
- Your company made **less than $1M USD in revenue last tax year**
- You're using it for personal projects, academic work, or open source

If any of the above is true, you don't need to contact me. Just use it. Keep the license file in the repo and the copyright notice. That's it.

## Commercial license required if

You need a paid commercial license if:

- Your company has **100 or more people**
- Your company made **$1M or more in revenue last tax year**
- You're building a product or service that uses ARN v9 under any of the above conditions

This includes:

- Embedding ARN v9 in a commercial product your company sells
- Running ARN v9 as part of a SaaS offering to customers
- Using ARN v9 internally at an enterprise
- Forking or adapting substantial parts of ARN v9 for commercial use

## How to get a commercial license

Open an issue on this repo titled "Commercial licensing inquiry" or reach me through the contact info on my GitHub profile. Include:

1. Your company name and size
2. How you want to use ARN v9 (internal tool, product, SaaS, etc.)
3. Your timeline

I'll respond within a few days with pricing. I'm a student so my pricing is reasonable — I'm not trying to gouge anyone, I just want to be paid fairly if a real business makes money using what I built.

## Why this license?

Some honest context on the choice:

I wanted ARN v9 to be free for regular people — students like me, hobbyists, researchers, small startups. But if Amazon or Google or some enterprise decides to build a product on top of this, or rip out the architecture for their own use, I should get paid for that. That's the whole point of PolyForm Small Business — it draws a clean line based on company size rather than making me guess or argue.

This isn't an "open source" license in the OSI sense — it's "source available." You can read the code, modify it, fork it, learn from it. But if you're a big company using it commercially, we need to talk first.
